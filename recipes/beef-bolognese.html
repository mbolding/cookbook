Beef Bolognese

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 2 hrs Serves: 6

Description

A rich, slow-cooked Italian meat sauce that gets better with every hour on the stove. Ground beef and pork simmered with wine, tomato, and a splash of milk create a velvety sauce that clings beautifully to thick pasta.

Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef
  • 250g ground pork
  • 1 onion, finely diced
  • 2 carrots, finely diced
  • 3 stalks celery, finely diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 150ml dry white wine
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 100ml whole milk
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt, pepper, and nutmeg to taste
  • 500g tagliatelle or pappardelle
  • Parmesan, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery. Cook for 8–10 minutes until very soft.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the ground beef and pork and cook, breaking it up, until browned.
  3. Pour in the wine and let it bubble until mostly evaporated, about 3 minutes.
  4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, bay leaf, and a grating of nutmeg. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Reduce heat to very low. Simmer uncovered for at least 1.5–2 hours, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of water if needed.
  6. Stir in the milk during the last 15 minutes of cooking.
  7. Cook pasta in well-salted water until al dente. Toss with the bolognese and serve with grated Parmesan.
