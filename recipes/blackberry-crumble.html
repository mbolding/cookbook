Bramble-Berry Crumble

🫐
Prep: 15 mins Cook: 40 mins Serves: 6

Description

Wild blackberries and tart apples beneath a buttery, golden oat crumble — the kind of pudding a hobbit would make after a foraging walk along the hedgerows of the Shire. Serve warm with a generous pour of custard or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Ingredients

  • Fruit filling: 400g blackberries (fresh or frozen), 2 Bramley apples peeled and sliced, 3 tbsp caster sugar, 1 tbsp lemon juice, 1 tsp cornstarch
  • Crumble topping: 150g plain flour, 100g cold butter cubed, 75g demerara sugar, 50g rolled oats, 1/2 tsp cinnamon, pinch of salt
  • Custard or vanilla ice cream, to serve

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Toss blackberries and sliced apples with sugar, lemon juice, and cornstarch. Spread into a baking dish.
  3. Make the crumble: Rub cold butter into flour with your fingertips until you have a mix of coarse and fine crumbs. Stir in demerara sugar, oats, cinnamon, and salt.
  4. Scatter the crumble loosely over the fruit — don't pack it down, you want craggy peaks and gaps where the juices can bubble through.
  5. Bake for 35–40 minutes until the topping is deep golden and the fruit juices are bubbling up at the edges, dark purple and irresistible.
  6. Rest for 10 minutes before serving. Spoon into bowls and drown in warm custard. Second helpings are mandatory — a hobbit tradition.
