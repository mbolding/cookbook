Wine-Braised Lamb Shanks à la Smade

Prep: 20 mins Cook: 3 hrs Serves: 4

Description

Lamb shanks braised for three patient hours until the meat yields at the merest suggestion of a fork — submerged in a dark reduction of red wine, aromatics, and herbs. A dish one might be served at a certain ancient tavern, where the proprietor's geniality is exceeded only by the excellence of his kitchen. The gravy alone warrants the journey.

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb shanks
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, roughly chopped
  • 3 carrots, cut into chunks
  • 4 stalks celery, chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 500ml robust red wine (a Côtes du Rhône or similar)
  • 500ml beef or lamb stock
  • 3 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 strip of orange peel
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Creamy polenta or mashed potato, to serve
  • Fresh parsley, chopped, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F (160°C). Season the lamb shanks liberally with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven over high heat. Sear the shanks until deeply browned on all sides — this requires patience and high heat, about 3–4 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  3. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for 6–8 minutes until beginning to caramelize. Add garlic and tomato paste; stir for 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in the red wine. Bring to a vigorous simmer and let reduce by one-third, about 5 minutes, scraping up all the dark fond from the bottom.
  5. Add stock, rosemary, thyme, bay leaves, and orange peel. Return the lamb shanks to the pot — the liquid should come about two-thirds up their sides.
  6. Cover tightly and transfer to the oven. Braise for 2.5–3 hours, turning the shanks once halfway through, until the meat is extraordinarily tender and pulling away from the bone.
  7. Remove the shanks and strain the braising liquid. Return the liquid to the pot and reduce over medium heat until it coats the back of a spoon — a rich, dark, glossy sauce.
  8. Serve each shank atop a mound of creamy polenta, spooned with the reduced sauce, and scattered with parsley. Pour the remaining wine into glasses of appropriate grandeur.
