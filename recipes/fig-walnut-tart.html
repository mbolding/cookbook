Honeyed Fig & Walnut Tart

🍒
Prep: 30 mins Cook: 35 mins Serves: 8

Description

A tart of considerable splendor: ripe figs arranged in concentric spirals atop a frangipane of walnuts and honey, the whole enclosed in a crisp, buttery shell. A confection that might conclude a feast of seven courses, accompanied perhaps by a digestif of ancient provenance and a discourse upon the nature of aesthetics.

Ingredients

  • Pastry: 200g plain flour, 100g cold butter cubed, 30g icing sugar, 1 egg yolk, 2 tbsp ice water, pinch of salt
  • Walnut frangipane: 100g walnuts finely ground, 80g butter softened, 80g caster sugar, 2 eggs, 1 tbsp flour, 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Topping: 8–10 ripe figs quartered, 3 tbsp honey, 1 tbsp butter, zest of 1 orange, pinch of cinnamon
  • Crème fraîche, to serve

Instructions

  1. Make the pastry: Pulse flour, butter, icing sugar, and salt in a processor until it resembles breadcrumbs. Add egg yolk and ice water; pulse until the dough just comes together. Flatten into a disc, wrap, and chill for 30 minutes.
  2. Roll out the pastry and line a 9-inch fluted tart tin. Prick the base with a fork. Blind bake at 375°F (190°C) with parchment and baking beans for 15 minutes. Remove beans, bake 5 more minutes until pale gold.
  3. Make the frangipane: Beat softened butter and sugar until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, then fold in ground walnuts, flour, and vanilla.
  4. Spread frangipane into the tart shell. Arrange quartered figs in concentric circles, pressing them gently into the filling.
  5. Bake at 350°F (180°C) for 30–35 minutes until the frangipane is golden and set.
  6. While warm, heat honey, butter, orange zest, and cinnamon together until bubbling. Brush generously over the figs and frangipane to create a lustrous glaze.
  7. Serve warm or at room temperature with a spoonful of crème fraîche. Accept praise with an air of practiced modesty.
