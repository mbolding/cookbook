Fish & Chips

🐟
Prep: 20 mins Cook: 30 mins Serves: 4

Description

The British pub classic, done right at home. Flaky white fish in a crisp, golden beer batter served alongside thick-cut chips with malt vinegar and tartar sauce. Best enjoyed with a generous squeeze of lemon.

Ingredients

  • 4 cod or haddock fillets (about 150g each)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour, plus extra for dredging
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup cold beer (lager works best)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 4 large potatoes, cut into thick chips
  • Salt and malt vinegar, for serving
  • Lemon wedges and tartar sauce, for serving

Instructions

  1. Cut potatoes into thick chips (about 1cm wide). Soak in cold water for 30 minutes, then drain and pat completely dry.
  2. Heat oil to 325°F (160°C) in a deep fryer or large heavy pot. Par-fry the chips for 5–6 minutes until cooked through but still pale. Remove and drain. Increase oil temperature to 375°F (190°C).
  3. While chips rest, make the batter: whisk flour, baking powder, and salt together. Pour in the cold beer and stir until just combined — a few lumps are fine.
  4. Pat the fish fillets dry and season with salt. Dust lightly in flour, then dip into the batter, letting excess drip off.
  5. Carefully lower the battered fish into the hot oil. Fry for 5–7 minutes, turning once, until deep golden and crispy. Drain on a wire rack.
  6. Return the chips to the hot oil and fry for 2–3 minutes until golden and crispy. Season immediately with salt.
  7. Serve the fish and chips with lemon wedges, tartar sauce, and a splash of malt vinegar.
