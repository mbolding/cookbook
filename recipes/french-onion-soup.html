French Onion Soup

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 1 hr 15 mins Serves: 4

Description

A deeply savory, soul-warming classic. Slow-caramelized onions in a rich beef broth, topped with a crusty baguette crouton and a golden, bubbly blanket of Gruyère cheese. The patience required is absolutely worth it.

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 4 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine or dry sherry
  • 1.5 liters beef broth
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 baguette, sliced into rounds
  • 200g Gruyère cheese, grated

Instructions

  1. Melt butter and olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add onions and a pinch of salt. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 45–60 minutes until deeply caramelized and golden brown. Add sugar halfway through to help caramelization.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add the wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Cook for 3 minutes until evaporated.
  3. Add the beef broth, thyme, and bay leaf. Bring to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes. Remove herbs. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Preheat broiler. Ladle soup into oven-safe bowls placed on a baking sheet.
  5. Float a baguette round on top of each bowl and pile generously with Gruyère.
  6. Broil for 3–5 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling with golden spots. Serve immediately and carefully — bowls will be very hot.
