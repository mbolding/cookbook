Margherita Pizza

Prep: 1 hr 30 mins Cook: 12 mins Serves: 2

Description

The queen of all pizzas — simple, elegant, and perfect. A pillowy, charred crust topped with San Marzano tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and fragrant basil. Proof that the best food needs only a few great ingredients.

Ingredients

  • 300g bread flour (or '00' flour)
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 200ml warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
  • 1 can (400g) San Marzano tomatoes, crushed by hand
  • 200g fresh mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Pinch of flaky sea salt

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, yeast, sugar, and salt. Add warm water and olive oil. Knead for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover and let rise for 1 hour until doubled.
  2. Preheat your oven as hot as it will go (ideally 500°F / 260°C) with a baking sheet or pizza stone inside.
  3. Punch down the dough and divide in two. Stretch each piece into a thin round on a floured surface.
  4. Season the crushed tomatoes with a pinch of salt. Spread a thin layer over each pizza, leaving a 1-inch border.
  5. Scatter torn mozzarella over the sauce.
  6. Transfer to the hot baking sheet/stone and bake for 10–12 minutes until the crust is puffed and charred at the edges.
  7. Top with fresh basil leaves, a drizzle of olive oil, and flaky salt. Slice and serve immediately.
