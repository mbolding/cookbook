Farmer Maggot's Mushroom & Ale Stew

🍄
Prep: 15 mins Cook: 1 hr Serves: 4

Description

A deep, dark, impossibly savory stew built on a mountain of mushrooms and a good pour of ale — the sort of dish a hobbit might dream about while on an unexpected adventure. Named for the Shire farmer whose mushrooms Pippin and Merry couldn't resist "borrowing."

Ingredients

  • 500g mixed mushrooms (button, cremini, oyster), thickly sliced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, chunked
  • 2 stalks celery, sliced
  • 2 medium potatoes, cubed
  • 330ml dark ale or stout
  • 2 cups vegetable or beef broth
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt, pepper, and a dash of Worcestershire sauce
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat butter and olive oil in a large heavy pot over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms in batches — don't crowd the pot — and cook until deeply golden, about 5–6 minutes per batch. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, cook onion, carrots, and celery for 5 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
  3. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir for 1 minute to cook it out.
  4. Pour in the ale and scrape up any brown bits from the bottom. Let it bubble for 2 minutes.
  5. Add broth, tomato paste, potatoes, thyme, and bay leaf. Return the mushrooms to the pot.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cook covered for 35–40 minutes until the potatoes are tender and the stew is thick and rich.
  7. Season with salt, pepper, and Worcestershire. Discard the bay leaf and thyme stems. Ladle into deep bowls and serve with thick slices of crusty bread.
