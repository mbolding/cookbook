Pad Thai

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 15 mins Serves: 2

Description

Thailand's most beloved noodle dish — chewy rice noodles stir-fried with shrimp, egg, crunchy bean sprouts, and a tangy tamarind-based sauce. Finished with a squeeze of lime and roasted peanuts.

Ingredients

  • 200g flat rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp, peeled (or firm tofu for vegetarian)
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 3 spring onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, crushed
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Dried chili flakes, to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak rice noodles in warm water for 20 minutes until pliable but not fully cooked. Drain and set aside.
  2. Mix together the fish sauce, tamarind paste, soy sauce, and sugar to make the sauce. Set aside.
  3. Heat oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds.
  4. Add shrimp and cook until pink, about 2 minutes. Push to one side.
  5. Crack eggs into the wok and scramble them, then mix into the shrimp.
  6. Add the drained noodles and pour the sauce over everything. Toss vigorously for 2–3 minutes until noodles absorb the sauce.
  7. Add bean sprouts and spring onions, and toss for another 30 seconds.
  8. Serve topped with crushed peanuts and lime wedges on the side.
