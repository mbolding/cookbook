Shire Pork Pie

Prep: 45 mins Cook: 1 hr 30 mins Serves: 6

Description

A proper raised pork pie with a hot-water crust pastry — the kind you'd find wrapped in cloth in a hobbit's second-breakfast basket. Golden, sturdy, and filled with seasoned pork. Equally good warm from the oven or cold the next day on a walk through the countryside.

Ingredients

  • Hot-water crust: 400g plain flour, 150g lard, 150ml water, 1 tsp salt, 1 egg (for glaze)
  • Filling: 600g pork shoulder, finely chopped (not minced), 150g unsmoked bacon, finely diced, 1 tsp sage, 1/2 tsp white pepper, 1/2 tsp allspice, 1/2 tsp salt, 1 small onion grated, splash of Worcestershire
  • Jelly (optional): 200ml chicken stock, 1 tsp gelatin powder

Instructions

  1. Make the pastry: Sift flour and salt into a bowl. Heat water and lard together until the lard melts and the mixture boils. Pour into the flour and mix quickly with a wooden spoon, then knead gently until smooth. Work quickly — this dough stiffens as it cools.
  2. Reserve a quarter of the dough for the lid. Press the rest into a greased 7-inch springform pan, working it up the sides evenly.
  3. Make the filling: Mix the chopped pork, bacon, sage, pepper, allspice, salt, grated onion, and Worcestershire. Pack tightly into the pastry case.
  4. Roll out the reserved dough for the lid. Place on top, crimp the edges to seal, and cut a small steam hole in the center. Brush with beaten egg.
  5. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 30 minutes, then reduce to 325°F (160°C) for a further 60 minutes. If the top browns too quickly, cover loosely with foil.
  6. Cool in the tin for 30 minutes. If making jelly: dissolve gelatin in warm stock, then carefully pour through the steam hole using a small funnel. Refrigerate overnight to set.
  7. Unmold and slice thickly. Serve with pickles and mustard, as any self-respecting hobbit would.
