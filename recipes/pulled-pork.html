Slow Cooker Pulled Pork

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 8 hrs Serves: 8

Description

Set it and forget it. A pork shoulder slow-cooked until it falls apart at the touch of a fork, then tossed in smoky barbecue sauce. Pile it high on soft brioche buns with coleslaw for the ultimate sandwich.

Ingredients

  • 1.5kg boneless pork shoulder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce, plus extra for serving
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • Brioche buns, for serving
  • Coleslaw, for serving

Instructions

  1. Mix brown sugar, smoked paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, cumin, cayenne, salt, and pepper together. Rub generously all over the pork shoulder.
  2. Place the pork in a slow cooker. Pour the chicken broth and apple cider vinegar around it.
  3. Cover and cook on LOW for 8 hours (or HIGH for 4–5 hours) until the meat is tender and shreds easily.
  4. Remove the pork and shred with two forks, discarding any large fat pieces.
  5. Drain most of the liquid from the slow cooker, leaving about 1/4 cup. Return the shredded pork and add the barbecue sauce. Toss to coat.
  6. Serve on toasted brioche buns topped with coleslaw and extra sauce.
