Almond & Rosewater Panna Cotta

Prep: 15 mins + chill Cook: 5 mins Serves: 6

Description

A trembling, ivory-pale custard perfumed with rosewater and almond — a dessert of ethereal delicacy that dissolves on the tongue like a half-remembered enchantment. Garnished with crushed pistachios and a drizzle of honey, it concludes a meal with precisely the right note of refined elegance. One's guests, if properly civilized, will express admiration in terms both florid and sincere.

Ingredients

  • 500ml double cream
  • 100ml whole milk
  • 75g caster sugar
  • 2 tsp rosewater (adjust to taste — too much and the dish tastes of soap)
  • 1/2 tsp almond extract
  • 2 1/2 tsp powdered gelatin (1 packet)
  • 3 tbsp cold water
  • Garnish: 2 tbsp crushed pistachios, 2 tbsp honey, dried rose petals (optional), pomegranate seeds

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle gelatin over cold water in a small bowl. Let it bloom for 5 minutes until spongy.
  2. In a saucepan, gently warm cream, milk, and sugar over medium-low heat, stirring until the sugar dissolves. Do not let it boil — patience is essential.
  3. Remove from heat. Stir in the bloomed gelatin until completely dissolved. Add rosewater and almond extract. Taste — the rosewater should be a suggestion, not a declaration.
  4. Strain through a fine sieve to ensure silky smoothness. Pour into 6 lightly oiled ramekins, glasses, or molds.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight. The panna cotta should tremble when gently shaken but hold its shape.
  6. To unmold: dip each ramekin briefly in hot water, run a thin knife around the edge, and invert onto a plate. Or simply serve in the glasses — elegance need not be laborious.
  7. Drizzle with honey, scatter with crushed pistachios and pomegranate seeds, and, if you possess them, a few dried rose petals for dramatic effect. Serve with an expression of studied indifference.
