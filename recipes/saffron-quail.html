Saffron-Poached Quail with Pomegranate Glaze

🪶
Prep: 25 mins Cook: 45 mins Serves: 4

Description

A dish of exquisite refinement — tender quail bathed in a saffron-gold poaching liquor, then finished with a lustrous pomegranate glaze. The sort of preparation one might encounter at the table of a magician of high estate, served on porcelain of surpassing delicacy alongside a wine of notable character. "The meal," one might observe, "achieves a certain adequacy."

Ingredients

  • 4 whole quail, semi-boneless
  • Large pinch of saffron threads (about 1/2 tsp)
  • 500ml chicken stock
  • 200ml dry white wine
  • 3 tbsp pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 shallot, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, sliced
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Seeds of 1 pomegranate, for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • Salt and white pepper
  • Couscous or saffron rice, to serve

Instructions

  1. Steep saffron in the warm chicken stock for 10 minutes to bloom its color and fragrance.
  2. Season quail generously with salt and white pepper. In a heavy pan, melt butter over medium-high heat and sear the quail on all sides until golden, about 2 minutes per side. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, soften the shallot for 2 minutes. Add garlic, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves; cook 1 minute.
  4. Pour in white wine and deglaze, scraping up the golden fond. Let reduce by half.
  5. Add the saffron-infused stock. Nestle the quail into the liquid. Cover and poach gently for 20–25 minutes until the quail are cooked through and tender.
  6. Remove the quail. Strain the poaching liquid and return it to the pan. Whisk in pomegranate molasses and honey. Reduce over medium heat until syrupy and glossy, about 8 minutes.
  7. Spoon the glaze over the quail. Scatter with fresh pomegranate seeds and torn mint. Serve atop saffron rice or pearl couscous — and accept the compliments with studied nonchalance.
