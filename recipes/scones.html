Elevenses Scones

🫖
Prep: 10 mins Cook: 15 mins Serves: 8

Description

Light, flaky, golden scones — the proper kind, served warm with clotted cream and strawberry jam. Essential for that most important of hobbit meals: elevenses. Between second breakfast and luncheon, a hobbit needs sustenance.

Ingredients

  • 350g self-raising flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 85g cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 3 tbsp caster sugar
  • 175ml whole milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 egg, beaten (for glaze)
  • Clotted cream and strawberry jam, to serve

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C). Line a baking tray with parchment.
  2. Sift flour, salt, and baking powder into a large bowl. Add the cold butter cubes and rub in with your fingertips until the mixture resembles coarse breadcrumbs.
  3. Stir in sugar. Make a well in the center and pour in milk and vanilla. Mix with a butter knife using cutting motions until a soft, shaggy dough forms. Do not knead or overwork — lumpy is fine.
  4. Turn out onto a floured surface and pat gently to about 2cm thick. Cut with a 6cm round cutter, pressing straight down without twisting.
  5. Place on the baking tray with edges almost touching. Brush tops with beaten egg.
  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes until risen, golden, and the sides look dry.
  7. Cool on a wire rack for a few minutes. Split open while still warm — never use a knife, pull apart gently — and pile with clotted cream and jam.
