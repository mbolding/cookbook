Bilbo's Seed Cake

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 45 mins Serves: 8

Description

The very seed cake that Bilbo Baggins served to a company of unexpected dwarves. A dense, buttery English cake fragrant with caraway seeds — perfectly paired with a pot of tea at elevenses. "I suppose you will all stay to supper?" he said in his politest unpressing tones.

Ingredients

  • 225g unsalted butter, softened
  • 225g caster sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 280g self-raising flour
  • 2 tbsp caraway seeds
  • 2 tbsp whole milk
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Demerara sugar, for the top

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (180°C). Grease and line a 9-inch round cake tin with parchment.
  2. Cream the butter and sugar together until pale and fluffy, about 4–5 minutes.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, adding a tablespoon of flour with each to prevent curdling.
  4. Fold in the remaining flour, caraway seeds, lemon zest, vanilla, milk, and salt. Mix gently until just combined — do not overmix.
  5. Pour the batter into the prepared tin and smooth the top. Sprinkle with demerara sugar for a crunchy crust.
  6. Bake for 40–45 minutes until a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean and the top is golden.
  7. Cool in the tin for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Slice generously — a hobbit would.
