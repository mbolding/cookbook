Shakshuka

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 25 mins Serves: 4

Description

Eggs gently poached in a spiced, smoky tomato and pepper sauce — straight from the skillet with crusty bread for dipping. A Middle Eastern and North African staple that works brilliantly for breakfast, brunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4–6 large eggs
  • Fresh cilantro or parsley, chopped
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper; cook for 5–6 minutes until softened.
  2. Add garlic, cumin, paprika, and chili flakes. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in crushed tomatoes and tomato paste. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
  4. Make small wells in the sauce and crack an egg into each one. Cover the skillet with a lid.
  5. Cook for 5–8 minutes until the egg whites are set but the yolks are still runny (or cook longer for firmer yolks).
  6. Remove from heat. Top with fresh herbs and crumbled feta if desired. Serve straight from the skillet with crusty bread.
