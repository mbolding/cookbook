Gaffer's Shepherd's Pie

🥘
Prep: 20 mins Cook: 45 mins Serves: 6

Description

A proper shepherd's pie as the Gaffer Gamgee himself would make — slow-cooked lamb beneath a cloud of buttery mashed potatoes, baked until the top turns golden and craggy. The definitive comfort food for a hobbit after a long day tending to their garden.

Ingredients

  • Filling: 500g ground lamb, 1 onion diced, 2 carrots diced, 1 cup frozen peas, 3 cloves garlic minced, 2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 cup beef or lamb broth, 2 tbsp flour, 1 tbsp fresh rosemary chopped, 2 sprigs thyme, salt and pepper
  • Topping: 1kg Yukon Gold potatoes peeled and cubed, 80g butter, 100ml warm milk, salt and white pepper, 1/2 cup shredded cheddar (optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil potatoes in salted water until fork-tender, about 15 minutes. Drain, then mash with butter, warm milk, salt, and white pepper until smooth and creamy. Set aside.
  2. Brown the lamb in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat, breaking it up as it cooks. Drain excess fat, leaving about 1 tablespoon.
  3. Add onion and carrots; cook 5 minutes. Add garlic, rosemary, and thyme; cook 1 minute.
  4. Sprinkle flour over the lamb and stir for 1 minute. Add tomato paste, Worcestershire, and broth. Simmer for 10 minutes until thickened.
  5. Stir in peas. Season with salt and pepper. Remove thyme stems.
  6. Spread mashed potatoes over the filling, starting from the edges and working inward. Use a fork to create peaks and ridges across the top. Scatter cheddar if using.
  7. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes until the top is golden and the filling bubbles around the edges. Let rest 10 minutes before serving — if you can wait that long.
