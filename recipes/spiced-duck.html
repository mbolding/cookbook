Spiced Duck with Orange & Star Anise

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 30 mins Serves: 2

Description

Pan-roasted duck breast scored in a diamond pattern and served in a dark, aromatic sauce of blood orange, star anise, and a whisper of five-spice. The skin, when properly rendered, achieves a shattering crispness that provides a most agreeable counterpoint to the rose-pink flesh within. A dish that demands precision, rewards patience, and pairs excellently with pretension.

Ingredients

  • 2 duck breasts (about 200g each)
  • 1/4 tsp Chinese five-spice powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • Juice of 2 blood oranges (or navel oranges)
  • Zest of 1 orange
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 shallot, finely sliced
  • 100ml chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • Sautéed greens or roasted root vegetables, to serve

Instructions

  1. Score the duck skin in a tight diamond crosshatch pattern, cutting through the fat but not into the flesh. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and five-spice.
  2. Place duck breasts skin-side down in a cold non-stick pan. Turn the heat to medium-low. Render the fat slowly for 12–15 minutes — do not rush this — until the skin is deeply golden and shatteringly crisp.
  3. Flip and cook flesh-side down for 3–4 minutes for medium-rare. Remove to a board and rest for 8 minutes (this is non-negotiable).
  4. Pour off all but 1 tablespoon of duck fat (save it — it's liquid gold). Add shallot and star anise; cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add orange juice, zest, honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, and stock. Simmer for 5–6 minutes until reduced to a glossy sauce that coats the back of a spoon.
  6. Remove star anise and swirl in butter to finish the sauce.
  7. Slice the duck breasts on the bias into thin medallions. Fan across the plate, spoon the orange sauce generously over the top, and serve alongside sautéed greens. Contemplate the fleeting nature of beauty while the dish is still warm.
