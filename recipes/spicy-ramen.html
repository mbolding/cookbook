Spicy Ramen

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 30 mins Serves: 2

Description

A deeply savory, spicy broth loaded with springy noodles, a soft-boiled egg, chashu pork, and vibrant toppings. This weeknight version delivers serious ramen-shop flavor without an all-day cook.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups chicken or pork broth
  • 2 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 200g fresh ramen noodles (or dried)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g pork belly slices or chashu pork
  • 2 scallions, sliced
  • 1 sheet nori, halved
  • Corn kernels, bamboo shoots, and bean sprouts (optional toppings)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • Sesame seeds, to garnish

Instructions

  1. Soft-boil eggs: bring water to a boil, gently lower eggs in, cook for exactly 6.5 minutes. Transfer to ice water, peel, and halve.
  2. In a saucepan over medium heat, sauté garlic and ginger in sesame oil for 1 minute. Add gochujang and cook for another minute.
  3. Add broth and bring to a simmer. Whisk in miso paste and soy sauce. Don't boil after adding miso. Keep warm.
  4. Cook noodles according to package directions. Drain.
  5. Pan-fry pork belly slices until caramelized, about 2–3 minutes per side.
  6. Divide noodles between two deep bowls. Ladle hot broth over each. Arrange pork, egg halves, scallions, nori, and your chosen toppings.
  7. Add a small knob of butter to each bowl. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve immediately.
