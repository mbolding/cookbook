Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 15 mins Serves: 2

Description

Glossy teriyaki-glazed salmon over a bowl of steamed rice with cucumber, edamame, and a drizzle of sesame. Fresh, balanced, and on the table in 25 minutes — a weeknight hero.

Ingredients

  • 2 salmon fillets (about 150g each)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 cup short-grain sushi rice, cooked
  • 1/2 cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup shelled edamame
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 scallions, sliced
  • Pickled ginger, to serve (optional)

Instructions

  1. Whisk together soy sauce, honey, mirin, sesame oil, ginger, and garlic to make the teriyaki sauce.
  2. Place salmon in a shallow dish and pour half the sauce over it. Marinate for 5–10 minutes.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high. Cook salmon skin-side down for 3–4 minutes, then flip. Pour the remaining sauce into the pan and cook for 2–3 more minutes, basting the salmon as the sauce reduces and glazes.
  4. Divide cooked rice between two bowls. Arrange cucumber, edamame, and avocado alongside the rice.
  5. Place a salmon fillet in each bowl. Spoon any remaining glaze over the top.
  6. Garnish with sesame seeds, scallions, and pickled ginger if desired. Serve immediately.
