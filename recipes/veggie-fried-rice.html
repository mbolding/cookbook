Vegetable Fried Rice

Prep: 10 mins Cook: 15 mins Serves: 4

Description

Better than takeout and ready in minutes. Day-old rice is the key to getting that perfect wok-fried texture. Loaded with colorful vegetables, sesame oil, and soy sauce, this is the ultimate fridge-clean-out meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked white or brown rice (day-old is ideal)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots, thawed
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 4 spring onions, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce (or extra soy sauce for vegetarian)
  • White pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a large wok or skillet over high heat until very hot. Add the vegetable oil.
  2. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  3. Add bell pepper, peas, carrots, and corn. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Push vegetables to the side and pour the beaten eggs into the center. Scramble until just set, then mix everything together.
  5. Add the rice, breaking up any clumps. Spread across the wok and press down; let it sit undisturbed for 1–2 minutes to get some crispy bits.
  6. Add soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sesame oil. Toss everything to combine well.
  7. Stir in spring onions, season with white pepper, and serve hot.
